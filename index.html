Nexus AI Unveils "Flow": An Ambient Productivity Tool Designed to Disappear Into Your Workflow

By Rachel Osman | TechPulse | June 14, 2025

San Francisco-based startup Nexus AI quietly launched its flagship product on Tuesday: a desktop application called Flow that promises to manage task prioritization, meeting summaries, and email triage without requiring the user to switch contexts.

Flow runs persistently in the background of a user's operating system, monitoring active windows and calendar events to build a real-time picture of what the user is working on. Rather than presenting a separate interface, the tool surfaces suggestions and automations as overlays that appear directly over the relevant application.

"We kept hearing from knowledge workers that every productivity tool just adds another thing to manage," said Priya Menon, Nexus AI's co-founder and CEO, speaking at the product's soft launch in San Francisco on Monday. "Flow is designed to not exist until you need it."

The launch follows an eighteen-month private beta with approximately 2,400 users across industries including finance, healthcare, and software development. Nexus AI claims beta users reported a 22 percent reduction in self-reported time spent managing communications, though the company has not published its methodology.

Flow's headline feature is what Nexus calls "passive meeting intelligence." When a calendar event begins, Flow automatically joins audio-permitted meetings, generates a timestamped summary, and flags action items attributed to the user. Action items are then surfaced in the user's preferred task management tool — currently supporting Notion, Linear, and Asana — without requiring manual entry.

The application also integrates with Gmail and Outlook to triage inbound email, labeling messages by urgency and drafting responses the user can approve in a single keystroke.

Nexus AI, which was founded in 2022 and has raised $34 million in Series A funding led by Benchmark Capital, faces competition from established players including Microsoft Copilot and Google Workspace's Duet AI, both of which offer overlapping features at enterprise scale.

Flow is currently available as a free beta download for macOS. A Windows version is expected in Q3 2025. The company has not announced pricing for the full release.
